# Protein Flapjacks

Simple flapjacks packed with protein — no blender, no fuss, just mix and bake.

## Nutrition (per flapjack)

- **Calories:** 260
- **Protein:** 18g
- **Carbohydrates:** 28g
- **Fat:** 9g

_Nutrition values are based on typical UK supermarket brands. Actual values may vary depending on brand and weight._

## Ingredients

- **90g** Optimum Nutrition Hydrolysed Whey Vanilla  
  _Substitute:_ Any flavoured whey protein (vanilla or caramel works best)
- **180g** rolled oats (e.g. Tesco Jumbo Oats)  
  _Substitute:_ Instant oats or oat flour (texture will change)
- **90g** Arla LactoFree semi-skimmed milk  
  _Substitute:_ Skimmed milk or any plant-based alternative
- **100g** natural peanut butter (e.g. Whole Earth or Meridian)  
  _Substitute:_ Almond butter, cashew butter, or tahini
- **80g** ripe banana, mashed (~1 medium)  
  _Substitute:_ 75g unsweetened apple sauce or 60g Greek yogurt
- **2 tbsp** honey or maple syrup  
  _Substitute:_ Zero-calorie syrup (will alter texture slightly)
- **1 tsp** vanilla extract (optional)
- Pinch of salt
- Optional: 30g dark chocolate chips or chopped nuts

## Instructions

1. **Preheat oven** to 180°C (160°C fan). Line a small baking tray (~20x20cm) with parchment paper.
2. **Mix wet:** In a bowl, stir together peanut butter, banana, milk, honey, and vanilla until smooth.
3. **Add dry:** Fold in oats, protein powder, salt, and optional extras like chocolate chips.
4. **Bake:** Spread the mixture into the lined tray. Press flat and evenly.
5. Bake for **18–22 minutes**, until golden at the edges. Let cool fully before slicing into 6 bars.

## Serving Ideas

- Store in fridge for up to 5 days
- Great pre-workout, in lunchboxes, or as a post-gym snack
- Drizzle with melted dark chocolate or protein spread for extra flavour
